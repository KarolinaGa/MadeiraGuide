Laurissilva Forest

  The Laurisilva of Madeira is a relict of a previously widespread laurel forest, and that is why it's on the UNESCO list of world heritage sites. It is the largest surviving area of laurel forest and is believed to be 90% primary forest. It contains a unique suite of plants and animals, including many endemic species such as the Madeira long-toed pigeon.
